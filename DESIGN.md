# DESIGN.md

## Visual Direction

The site should feel like a 2026 field guide: animated, layered, technical, and approachable. Use a focused single-column learning path with expressive hero art, AI-system diagrams, and subtle motion.

## Physical Scene

A student opens the site before a live AI workshop on a laptop in a classroom, curious but slightly overwhelmed, looking for a map of what matters now.

## Color Strategy

Full palette. Keep the base in ink-blue and paper-blue, then use electric cyan, signal blue, violet, and warm amber as named accents. Avoid pure black and pure white. Avoid generic neon overload.

## Typography

Use strong display scale for the landing page, with high contrast between small field-guide labels and large headlines. Body line length should stay readable at 65-75ch.

## Layout

No side tabs for the landing page. Use one focused content column with hero art, animated AI map, and lesson path rows. Avoid nested boxes. Cards are allowed only when they represent a distinct object, such as a visual artifact or module tile.

## Motion

Use CSS-only motion for ambient AI-system visuals: slow orbiting nodes, scanning lines, soft pulses, and staggered entrance feel. Respect reduced-motion preferences.

## Imagery

Use purposeful generated/local vector imagery for AI concepts: model constellations, toolchain map, terminal/workflow panels, and safety signals. Do not use robot stock imagery.
